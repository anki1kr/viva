03Indian Mathematics & Astronomy

Zero · place value · Vedic sutras · Sulbasutras · jya (sine) · Aryabhata–Bhaskara lineage.

⭐ Broadest unit — 3 PYQ shorts + 1 5-marker

3.1 Why Indian mathematics matters

Concept Indian math gave the world the decimal place-value system, the concept of zero (shunya), foundations of algebra (bijaganita) & trigonometry (jyotpatti), and infinite series for π & sine — centuries before Newton/Leibniz.

3.3 Decimal & Zero ⭐

  • Place value — Aryabhata: "sthana sthana dasha-gunam syat" (each place is 10× previous).
  • Shunya (zero) — Brahmagupta first treated as a number with rules: a+0=a, a×0=0, a−0=a.
  • Negative numbers — Brahmagupta named positive dhana (fortune), negative rina (debt) — full sign rules.
  • Brahmi numerals → Indo-Arabic → today's 0–9.

3.4 Vedic Mathematics — 16 Sutras ⭐ 5M

Concept Reconstructed by Swami Bharati Krishna Tirtha (1884–1960). 16 sutras + 13 sub-sutras — one-line aphorisms turning long arithmetic into mental shortcuts.

Top 4 to memorise

SutraMeaningUse
Ekadhikena Purvena"by one more than previous"squares ending in 5
Nikhilam Navatashcaramam"all from 9, last from 10"multiply near 10/100/1000
Urdhva-Tiryagbhyam"vertically & crosswise"general multiplication
Paravartya Yojayet"transpose & apply"division

Worked example — 75² (Ekadhikena)

75² = 7×(7+1) | 25
    = 7×8     | 25
    = 56      | 25
    = 5625

Rule: N5² = N×(N+1) | 25
35² = 3×4 | 25 = 1225
85² = 8×9 | 25 = 7225

Worked example — 12 × 13 (Urdhva-Tiryagbhyam)

     1   2
     1   3
    ─── ─── ───
    1×1  (1×3)+(2×1)  2×3
     1        5         6
   = 156

"vertically & crosswise"
multiply ends · sum cross · multiply units

3.5 Sulbasutras & Baudhayana theorem

Concept Sulbasutras (~800 BCE) = appendices to Kalpa-Vedanga teaching priests how to build vedi (fire altars) of precise area. Earliest statement of Pythagoras theorem.
Baudhayana 1.12 (~800 BCE) "Dirghachatursrasyaaksnaya rajjuh parshvamani tiryagmani cha yat prithagbhute kurutastadubhayam karoti."
→ "Diagonal-rope of an oblong = sum of breadth-rope & length-rope (in area)" → a² + b² = c², well before Pythagoras.
  • Construction of squares, circles, trapezia of given area
  • √2 ≈ 1 + 1/3 + 1/(3·4) − 1/(3·4·34) ≈ 1.41421568 (correct to 5 decimals)
  • Squaring the circle (approximate)

3.6 Trigonometry — sine & jya

  • Aryabhata named the half-chord ardha-jya → jya → jiba (Arab) → sinus (Latin) → sine. The word "sine" is etymologically Sanskrit.
  • Aryabhata's jya table for every 3.75° from 0° to 90°.
  • Bhaskara II — sine-of-sums & half-angle formulas.
  • Madhava (~1400 CE) — infinite series: 
    π/4   = 1 − 1/3 + 1/5 − 1/7 + ...   (Madhava–Leibniz)
sin x = x − x³/3! + x⁵/5! − ...     (Madhava, before Newton)

4.1 What is Ayurveda?

Definition Ayuh (life) + Veda (knowledge) = "science of life." Indigenous Indian medicine, considered Upaveda of the Atharvaveda; oral form ~1500 BCE.

Brihat Trayi (Great Triad)

  • Charaka Samhita — Charaka (~1st c. CE) — internal medicine (Kayachikitsa)
  • Sushruta Samhita — Sushruta (~6th c. BCE) — surgery (Shalya-tantra) · "father of surgery"
  • Ashtanga Hridaya & Sangraha — Vagbhata (~7th c. CE) — concise compendia

5.3 Vastu Purusha Mandala

Concept Square grid (8×8=64 or 9×9=81 padas) on the plot. Cosmic Vastu Purusha lies face-down inside it. 45 deities (32 outer + 13 inner). Centre 9 squares = Brahmasthana, kept open.

5.4 Residential prescriptions

  1. Plot shape — square > rectangle > trapezoid; avoid triangle/cut-corner
  2. Slope — SW high → NE low (water drains NE)
  3. Soil test (bhumi-pariksha) — dig pit, refill: overflows = good · falls short = bad
  4. Orientation — main entry E or N, never SW
  5. Rooms — kitchen SE, bed SW, pooja NE, study NE/E
  6. Brahmasthana — central square open · never block with toilet/staircase
  7. Heights — SW corner tallest, NE shortest

Universal features of every IKS city

  • Surrounded by fortification (durga) + moat
  • Four cardinal gates aligned N-S-E-W
  • Central palace + temple at Brahmasthana
  • Zoning by occupation — Brahmins N, Kshatriyas E, Vaishyas S, artisans W
  • Wide raja-marga crossing E-W & N-S
  • Water bodies in NE · cremation grounds far S outside walls

Famous historical examples

  • Harappa & Mohenjo-daro (~2500 BCE) — earliest grid · drainage · citadel
  • Madurai — concentric rings around Meenakshi temple = Padmaka / Sarvatobhadra
  • Jaipur (1727) — designed on 9-pada Vastu Mandala by Vidyadhar Bhattacharya
  • Pataliputra — Megasthenes describes parallelogram on Ganges = Karmuka / Prastara

6.4 Mandala & Shadgunya — foreign policy

Mandala doctrine Concentric circles around the king (Vijigishu). Immediate neighbour = natural enemy; neighbour's neighbour = natural friend.

7.1 One-mark quick answers (Section A)

Who wrote the Arthashastra?

Kautilya / Chanakya / Vishnugupta (~4th c. BCE) — chief minister to Chandragupta Maurya.

Name the Saptanga.

Swami · Amatya · Janapada · Durga · Kosha · Danda · Mitra

The 4 Upayas?

Sama (conciliation) · Dana (gift) · Bheda (division) · Danda (force)

Three doshas?

Vata (air+ether) · Pitta (fire+water) · Kapha (earth+water)

Father of Indian surgery?

Sushruta — Sushruta Samhita · 300+ procedures · 120 instruments · rhinoplasty

Who introduced zero?

Brahmagupta (628 CE, Brahmasphutasiddhanta) — first to define rules of arithmetic with zero.

Traditional Vastu measurement unit?

Hasta (cubit) ≈ 24 angulas ≈ 45 cm. Ladder: angula → vitasti → hasta → danda → krosha → yojana.

8 branches of Ashtanga Ayurveda?

Kayachikitsa · Shalya · Shalakya · Kaumarabhritya · Agada · Bhuta-vidya · Rasayana · Vajikarana

3 temple architectural styles?

Nagara (North · curvilinear shikhara) · Dravida (South · pyramidal vimana) · Vesara (Deccan · hybrid)

Contribution of Varahamihira?

Pancha-Siddhantika · Brihat Samhita · Brihat Jataka — synthesised 5 astronomical schools

How many Vedic-Math sutras?

16 sutras + 13 sub-sutras · compiled by Bharati Krishna Tirtha (publ. 1965)

What is Sulbasutra contribution?

~800 BCE geometry texts · earliest Pythagoras theorem (Baudhayana 1.12) · √2 to 5 decimals · altar construction

State the Baudhayana theorem.

"Dirghachatursrasyaaksnaya rajjuh parshvamani tiryagmani cha yat prithagbhute kurutastadubhayam karoti" — square on diagonal = sum of squares on the two sides (Pythagoras, ~800 BCE).

What is Trividha Pariksha?

Three-fold Ayurvedic diagnosis: Darshana (inspection) · Sparshana (palpation) · Prashna (questioning).

Who wrote the Charaka Samhita?

Charaka — foundational Ayurvedic text on internal medicine (Kayachikitsa); part of Brihat-Trayi.

Who wrote the Ashtanga Hridaya?

Vagbhata (~7th c. CE) — concise summary of Charaka & Sushruta · part of Brihat-Trayi.

What is Aryabhatiya?

Mathematical-astronomical treatise (499 CE) by Aryabhata · sine table · π ≈ 3.1416 · Earth's rotation · place-value notation.

Bhaskara II's famous arithmetic text?

Lilavati — part of Siddhanta Shiromani (1150 CE) · arithmetic, algebra & mensuration.

Name the Shadgunya (6 foreign-policy measures).

Sandhi (peace) · Vigraha (war) · Asana (neutrality) · Yana (march) · Samshraya (shelter) · Dvaidhibhava (dual policy).

Name 3 classical Vastu texts.

Mayamatam (Maya · 9th c.) · Manasara (most exhaustive · 70 chapters) · Samarangana Sutradhara (King Bhoja · 11th c.).

What is Brahmasthana?

Central 9 padas of the Vastu Purusha Mandala — sacred space kept open as courtyard / skylight. Symbolises navel of cosmic Vastu-Purusha.

What is the kuttaka method?

Aryabhata's algorithm for solving linear indeterminate equations ax + by = c in integers (Diophantine). Anticipates the Extended Euclidean Algorithm.

Name the Brihat Trayi.

"Great Triad" of Ayurveda: Charaka Samhita · Sushruta Samhita · Ashtanga Hridaya (Vagbhata).

7.2 Four-mark mini answers

Q · 4M structure Define (1) → 2-3 features (2) → one example or modern parallel (1) = 4 marks.

Define Nitishastra. Name 3 major Niti texts.

Nitishastra = science of right conduct (niti). Texts: Niti-sara (Kamandaka) · Vidura Niti (Mahabharata) · Chanakya Niti · Niti-shataka (Bhartrihari) · Shukra Niti. Themes: control of arishadvarga, charity (dana), self-restraint (dama). Modern parallel = ethical leadership.

Explain Tridosha theory.

Health = balance of Vata (air+ether · movement) · Pitta (fire+water · digestion) · Kapha (earth+water · structure). Imbalance (vikriti) → disease. Each individual's unique mix = prakriti; treatment is personalised. "Vata moves, Pitta cooks, Kapha holds."

What is Vastu Purusha Mandala?

Sacred grid (8×8=64 or 9×9=81 padas) on building plot. Cosmic Vastu Purusha lies inside; 45 deities occupy specific squares. Centre 9 squares = Brahmasthana kept open. NE pooja · SE kitchen · SW master bed · NW guests.

Highlight contribution of Patanjali, Agastya, Kanada.

Patanjali — Yoga Sutras (Ashtanga Yoga) + Mahabhashya on Panini's grammar. Agastya — Vedic seer, Tamil Sangam grammarian, Agastya Samhita (medicine + siddha). Kanada — founder of Vaisheshika philosophy · atomic theory (paramanu) ~600 BCE · 2400 yrs before Dalton.

Saptanga theory of the State.

Definition: Kautilya's doctrine — state = body with 7 organs · weakness of any limb weakens the whole.
• Swami (king) · Amatya (ministers) · Janapada (territory + people)
• Durga (fort) · Kosha (treasury) · Danda (army & justice) · Mitra (allies)
• Mnemonic: "Sw-Am-Ja-Du-Ko-Da-Mi" · Source: Arthashastra, Book VI.
Modern parallel: nation-state with 7 functional pillars.

Four Upayas of Kautilya.

Definition: Four diplomatic instruments in escalating order.
• Sama — conciliation · sweet words
• Dana — gift · concession · bribery
• Bheda — sowing dissent inside rival camp
• Danda — punishment / force (last resort)
Modern parallel: diplomacy → aid → covert ops → sanctions/war.

Vastu Shastra — short note.

Definition: Indian science of architecture aligning buildings with cosmic forces, 5 elements & cardinal directions.
• Built on Vastu-Purusha mandala (9×9 = 81 padas)
• Direction logic: NE→water · SE→fire · SW→earth · NW→air · centre = Brahmasthana
• Texts: Mayamatam, Manasara, Samarangana Sutradhara
Application: Brihadeshwara temple plan · Jaipur grid (1727).

Traditional Vastu measurement system.

Definition: Modular human-scale system using body parts → buildings proportionate to occupants.
• Angula — finger-width (~1.9 cm) · base unit
• Hasta (cubit) = 24 angulas (~45 cm)
• Danda = 4 hastas · Krosha = 1000 dandas · Yojana = 4 krosha (~14.6 km)
• Tala = face-length, used in iconometry.

Three temple architectural styles.

Definition: Indian temples classified by tower form & region.
• Nagara — North · curvilinear shikhara · Khajuraho, Konark
• Dravida — South · pyramidal vimana + gopurams · Brihadeshwara, Meenakshi
• Vesara — Deccan · hybrid star-shaped · Hoysaleshwara (Halebidu)
All follow Vastu mandala & panchayatana layout.

Aryabhata's contributions.

Definition: 5th-c. CE astronomer-mathematician (b. 476 CE), author of Aryabhatiya.
• π ≈ 3.1416 (correct to 4 decimals)
• Earth rotates on its axis (heliocentric-like)
• Place-value system & sine table (jya) · roots of trigonometry
• Eclipses by shadow geometry (not Rahu-Ketu myth) · kuttaka algorithm.

Sushruta & Indian surgery.

Definition: ~6th c. BCE surgeon · author of Sushruta Samhita · "Father of Surgery".
• 300+ surgical procedures & 120+ instruments
• Pioneered rhinoplasty (basis of modern plastic surgery)
• Detailed cataract surgery, caesarean section, fracture management
• Classified surgery into 8 types: Chedya · Lekhya · Vedhya...

Vedic mathematics — overview.

Definition: System of high-speed mental arithmetic compiled from Atharvaveda by Bharati Krishna Tirtha (Shankaracharya of Puri, publ. 1965).
• 16 sutras + 13 sub-sutras covering all 4 operations
• "Ekadhikena Purvena" — squares ending in 5 (e.g. 75²=5625)
• "Urdhva-Tiryagbhyam" — single-line multiplication

A3-Q1. Analyze the significance of spherical geometry & early calculus concepts in Indian astronomy. (8M)

Why these mattered: Indian astronomers worked on the celestial sphere, not flat geometry · planetary orbits, eclipses & declination demand spherical trig & rate-of-change concepts.
Spherical geometry contributions:
• Aryabhata (~499 CE) — direction-cosines for planetary positions · sine table for spherical trig (every 3.75°)

Early calculus seeds:
• Bhaskara II's tatkalika gati — "instantaneous velocity" · the planet's instantaneous speed at any moment · a derivative in spirit
• Mean Value Theorem precursor — Bhaskara II proved that if a planet's true longitude equals mean longitude at two points, its rate of change vanishes between them
• Madhava (Kerala school, ~1400 CE) — infinite series for sin(x), cos(x), arctan(x) · "siddhanta-darpana" · 250+ years before Newton/Leibniz

π/4   = 1 − 1/3 + 1/5 − 1/7 + ...   (Madhava–Leibniz)
sin x = x − x³/3! + x⁵/5! − ...     (Madhava series)
• Nilakantha (Tantrasangraha) — convergence acceleration techniques · partial heliocentric model
Conclude: spherical geometry & calculus seeds proved that Indian astronomy was mathematically sophisticated · the Kerala-school series is now recognised as an independent precursor to modern calculus.

A3-Q3. Explain the development of arithmetic, geometry & trigonometry in ancient India. (8M)

A3-Q4. Apply Vedic Sutras to solve arithmetic problems with suitable examples. (8M)

Show 4 sutras with worked examples:
1. Ekadhikena Purvena ("by one more than the previous") — squares of numbers ending in 5: 

75² = 7×(7+1) | 25 = 7×8 | 25 = 5625
35² = 3×4 | 25 = 1225
85² = 8×9 | 25 = 7225
95² = 9×10 | 25 = 9025
2. Urdhva-Tiryagbhyam ("vertically & crosswise") — general multiplication of any two numbers: 
     12              23
   × 13            × 14
   ───              ───
  Step: ends, cross, units
   12 × 13 = 1 | (1×3+2×1) | 6 = 1 | 5 | 6 = 156
   23 × 14 = 2 | (2×4+3×1) | 12 = 2 | 11 | 2 = 322
3. Nikhilam Navatashcaramam Dashatah ("all from 9 & last from 10") — multiplication near a base of 10/100/1000: 
97 × 96   (base 100)
 97 → −3 (deficiency from 100)
 96 → −4
 cross-sum: 97−4 = 93   (or 96−3 = 93)
 product of deficiencies: 3×4 = 12
 → 93 | 12 = 9312
4. Paravartya Yojayet ("transpose & apply") — division & algebraic manipulation: 
Divide 1234 by 112 (base 100)
  divisor 112 = base 100 + 12, transpose to −12
  1234:  1 | 2 | 3  4
  carry: −12 × 1 = −12 → place under 23
  partial:  1  2−1 = 1  3−2=1  remainder
  → quotient ≈ 11, remainder 2
Significance: these sutras compress multi-step arithmetic to one-line mental shortcuts · faster than long multiplication · used in CAT/SAT, FPGA Vedic multipliers, cryptography accelerators.
