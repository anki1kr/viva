01 Economics, Management & Governance Unit VI · 4 Hrs

Unit VI is the highest-leverage unit — May-2025 had two Part-B questions (10 + 5 + 15 = 30 marks) from here, plus 2 short questions. Master the Arthashastra, Nitishastra, and the leadership-finance principles, and you instantly secure a passing block.

1.1 The Big Picture — two pillars of ancient Indian polity

TextAuthorEraFocusKey idea
ArthashastraKautilya / Chanakya / Vishnugupta~4th century BCEStatecraft, economy, war, taxation"Material welfare (artha) is the foundation of dharma & kama."
NitishastraKamandaka, Shukracharya, Vidura, BhartrihariVariousEthics, conduct, morality, leadership"Right conduct (niti) sustains the king and the kingdom."
Memory hook "Artha = Action; Niti = Norms" — Arthashastra tells the king what to do (taxation, army, spies). Nitishastra tells him how to behave (ethics, virtues, restraint).

1.2 Arthashastra — Kautilya's manual of statecraft

Definition The Arthashastra is a treatise on artha (material wealth, governance, economy) authored by Kautilya (also called Chanakya or Vishnugupta) — chief minister to Chandragupta Maurya. It contains 15 books (adhikaranas), 150 chapters, 180 sections, ~6000 sutras.

Saptanga — the 7 limbs (organs) of the State

The most famous Kautilyan model. State = body with 7 organs; if any limb weakens, the state weakens.

3JanapadaTerritory + populationGeography & citizens
4DurgaFortified capitalDefence infrastructure
5KoshaTreasuryPublic finance / RBI
6DandaArmy, force, justiceDefence + judiciary
7MitraAlliesForeign relations / treaties
Mnemonic — SAJ-DKDM "Swami's Amatya runs Janapada from Durga, holds Kosha, wields Danda, keeps Mitra."

The four Upayas — instruments of policy

The Four Upayas — Escalation Ladder
Escalate downward only when the gentler tool fails.

Mandala theory — circle of states (foreign policy)

Definition Kautilya's Mandala arranges states in concentric circles around the king (Vijigishu). The immediate neighbour is the natural enemy; the neighbour's neighbour is the natural friend.
Mandala — Concentric Circles of States
Rule: the immediate neighbour is the natural enemy; the neighbour's neighbour is the natural friend.

Public finance & taxation

  • Bali, Bhaga, Kara — three classes of revenue (tribute, share of produce, taxes).
  • Land tax: typically 1/6th of produce (shadbhaga).
  • Saptanga Kosha rule: treasury must always cover at least the cost of the army for one year.
  • Welfare expenditure mandated: relief for orphans, widows, the sick, & disaster victims (drought, flood, famine).

1.3 Nitishastra — the science of right conduct

Definition Nitishastra = science (shastra) of niti (right conduct, ethics, prudence). It complements Arthashastra: where Artha says "do this to win," Niti says "do this because it is right."

1.4 Leadership & motivation in ancient Indian texts

Rajadharma — duties of a leader

  1. Praja-palana — protection of subjects (highest duty).
  2. Atmavanigraha — self-control over the six enemies (arishadvarga: kama, krodha, lobha, moha, mada, matsarya).
  3. Vinaya — humility & willingness to learn (Kautilya: "Vinaya is the root of discipline.").
  4. Yoga-kshema — securing what is not yet acquired, preserving what is acquired (Bhagavad Gita 9.22).
  5. Dama, Daya, Dana — restraint, compassion, charity.

Motivation — the Trivarga & Purushartha framework

Definition The Purusharthas are the four legitimate aims of human life — Dharma (righteousness), Artha (wealth), Kama (desire), Moksha (liberation). Kautilya recommends a leader balance the first three (trivarga); Moksha is the spiritual culmination.
The Four Purusharthas
Trivarga (first three) is for the householder; Moksha is the spiritual culmination.

Bhagavad Gita on motivation (Karma Yoga)

Excerpt — Gita 2.47 "Karmanye vadhikaraste ma phaleshu kadachana..."
"You have a right to perform action, never to its fruits." — the foundational principle of nishkama karma (action without attachment to reward), still cited in modern leadership literature.

1.5 Planning, organising & financial management

Planning organs in Arthashastra

BodyFunction
MantriparishadCouncil of ministers — strategic decision-making
Adhyakshas (~30+ heads)Departmental superintendents (mines, mint, agriculture, weights, prostitutes, etc.)
SannidhataTreasurer — receipts & storage
SamahartaCollector-General — revenue collection & audit
AkshapatalaRecords office — accounts & audit

Financial management principles

  • Aya-Vyaya (income–expenditure) accounting must be reviewed daily.
  • Maintain three reserves in the treasury: routine, contingency, and dharma-charity.
  • Audit (samkhyana) is independent of the collector — early principle of separation of duties.
  • Coinage standardised — Pana (silver), Mashaka (copper), Suvarna (gold).
  • Punishment for embezzlement scales with rank — higher the office, harsher the penalty.
Modern relevance — exam favourite Saptanga ≈ modern state pillars; Mandala ≈ realpolitik; Sama-Dana-Bheda-Danda ≈ negotiation theory; Aya-Vyaya ≈ double-entry accounting; nishkama karma ≈ servant-leadership.

Quick Quiz — Unit VI (highest yield)

Q1. Who was Vishnugupta and what was his contribution? PYQ 2025 · 1.5
Vishnugupta is another name for Kautilya / Chanakya, chief minister to Chandragupta Maurya (~4th c. BCE). Contribution: authored the Arthashastra — a 15-book treatise on statecraft, economics, taxation, war & foreign policy. Defined the Saptanga theory of state, Mandala doctrine of foreign policy, and the Sama-Dana-Bheda-Danda upayas.
Q2. Elaborate on Indian economic thought as presented in Arthashastra. PYQ · 10 marks
Structure your answer in 5 blocks:
  1. Author + scope — Kautilya, 15 books, written for Chandragupta Maurya, treats artha as the foundation of dharma.
  2. Saptanga state model — Swami, Amatya, Janapada, Durga, Kosha, Danda, Mitra; Kosha (treasury) is central.
  3. Public finance — Bali, Bhaga, Kara; land tax 1/6th; departmental Adhyakshas; Samaharta & Sannidhata; daily aya-vyaya audit.
  4. Welfare economics — relief for widows, orphans, sick; famine & disaster funds; price control via mint & weight superintendents.
  5. Foreign policy + 4 Upayas — Mandala theory; Sama-Dana-Bheda-Danda as policy levers.
Conclude: Arthashastra anticipates modern public-finance (separation of duties, audit, welfare expenditure) by 2000+ years.
Q3. Write a short note on Nitishastra. PYQ · 5 marks
Nitishastra = science of right conduct (niti). Complements Arthashastra by stressing ethics, virtue & morality over pure pragmatism. Major texts: Niti-sara (Kamandaka), Vidura Niti (Mahabharata), Shukra Niti, Chanakya Niti, Niti-shataka (Bhartrihari). Themes: control of arishadvarga (six enemies — lust, anger, greed, delusion, pride, envy), Rajadharma, charity (dana), self-restraint (dama). Today it informs ethical leadership & values-based management.
Q4. Discuss the principles of leadership, motivation, and financial management in ancient Indian texts and their relevance today. PYQ · 15 marks
Three-section essay:
  • Leadership (Rajadharma) — Swami's qualities: vinaya, atma-vanigraha, praja-palana; Saptanga organs; Vidura's qualities of a leader. Modern parallel: servant leadership (Greenleaf), stakeholder model.
  • Motivation — Purushartha framework (Dharma–Artha–Kama–Moksha); Bhagavad Gita Karma Yoga (2.47): nishkama karma. Modern parallel: intrinsic motivation, self-determination theory.
  • Financial management — Kosha discipline, Aya-Vyaya, Samaharta vs Sannidhata (separation of duties), three reserves. Modern parallel: double-entry accounting, internal audit, contingency reserves.
End with relevance: Indian PSU governance, Ratan Tata-style ethical leadership, ESG, conscious capitalism — all echo Kautilya & Bhartrihari.
Q5. What are the four upayas of Kautilya?
Sama (conciliation), Dana (gift), Bheda (division), Danda (force). Try in this order — force is the last resort.
Q6. What are the seven limbs of the state (Saptanga)?
Swami, Amatya, Janapada, Durga, Kosha, Danda, Mitra — the king, ministers, territory, fortifications, treasury, army, and allies.

02 Indian Architecture & Planning Unit V · 3 Hrs

Vastushastra is the science of built environment. May-2025 asked it as a 10-mark city-planning question and a 1.5-mark short on measurement. Two clean concepts to memorise: the measurement ladder, and the eight city-types.

2.1 Vastushastra — what & why

Definition Vastushastra (literally "science of dwelling") is the ancient Indian system of architecture, urban planning & spatial design. Its principles harmonise human dwellings with the five elements (panchamahabhuta), cardinal directions (dik), and the movement of the sun.

2.3 The Vastu Purusha Mandala — sacred grid

Definition The Vastu Purusha Mandala is a square grid (typically 8×8 = 64 or 9×9 = 81 padas) overlaid on the building plot. The cosmic being Vastu Purusha lies face-down inside it, and 45 deities (32 outer + 13 inner) occupy specific squares. Centre 9 squares = Brahmasthana, kept open.
Vastu Purusha Mandala — 9-Square Grid
Brahmasthana (centre) stays open. NE = water/pooja; SE = fire/kitchen; SW = heavy/master bed; NW = guest.

2.4 Residential Vastu — prescriptions

  1. Plot shape: square > rectangle > trapezoid; avoid triangular & cut-corner plots.
  2. Slope: ground should slope from SW (high) → NE (low) so water drains NE.
  3. Soil test (bhumi-pariksha): dig a pit & refill — if soil overflows, plot is auspicious; if it falls short, inauspicious.
  4. Orientation: main entry typically east or north; never south-west.
  5. Rooms: kitchen SE, master bedroom SW, pooja NE, study NE/E.
  6. Brahmasthana: central square kept open — courtyard or skylight, never blocked by toilet/staircase.
  7. Heights: SW corner of building should be tallest, NE shortest.

2.5 City planning — eight classical patterns (PYQ — 10 marks)

Vastushastra (especially Manasara & Mayamatam) prescribes 8 town layouts, each suited to terrain, population, & purpose:

2SarvatobhadraSquare, four gates (one on each side), perfect symmetryCapital cities
3NandyavartaPlan resembling the nandyavarta flower (auspicious knot)Towns of nobility
4PadmakaLotus-shaped, concentric rings, hill-fortDefensive citadels

6PrastaraLong rectangle with parallel streets & cross lanesTrading towns / ports

Mnemonic — DSNPSPKC "Dandaka Sarvatobhadra Nandyavarta Padmaka Swastika Prastara Karmuka Chaturmukha" → "Dogs Sleep Near Pretty Sleepy Pups Kept Calm."

Common features of every IKS city plan

  • Surrounded by fortification (durga) with moat.
  • Four cardinal gates aligned to N-S-E-W.
  • Central royal palace + temple at the Brahmasthana.
  • Zoning by varna / occupation — Brahmins north, Kshatriyas east, Vaishyas south, Shudras & artisans west (zoning, not caste-bias as is often misread).
  • Wide raja-marga (king's road) crossing E-W & N-S.
  • Water bodies in the NE; cremation grounds far south outside the wall.

Famous historical examples

Harappa & Mohenjo-daro

Earliest grid plan (~2500 BCE) — straight streets, drainage, citadel + lower town. Pre-dates classical Vastu but follows the same instinct.

Madurai (Pandya capital)

Concentric square rings with the Meenakshi temple at centre — textbook Padmaka / Sarvatobhadra.

Jaipur (Pink City, 1727)

Designed by Vidyadhar Bhattacharya on a 9-square Vastu Purusha Mandala — modern proof of IKS town planning.

Pataliputra (Maurya capital)

Megasthenes' Indica describes a parallelogram on the Ganges — closest to the Karmuka / Prastara pattern.

Quick Quiz — Unit V

Q1. What is the traditional measurement system used in Vastushastra? PYQ 2025 · 1.5
An anthropometric ladder derived from the human body: Paramanu → Yava → Angula → Vitasti → Hasta → Danda → Krosha → Yojana. The Hasta (cubit, ~ 45 cm = 24 angulas) is the principal building unit; Yojana (~14.6 km) is used for inter-city distances.
Q2. Explain the principles of city planning in ancient India as per Vastushastra. PYQ · 10 marks
Frame in 4 layers:
  1. Foundation — Vastushastra texts (Mayamatam, Manasara, Brihat-Samhita); use of Vastu Purusha Mandala grid; anthropometric measurement.
  2. Eight city patterns — name & describe Dandaka, Sarvatobhadra, Nandyavarta, Padmaka, Swastika, Prastara, Karmuka, Chaturmukha.
  3. Universal rules — fortification (durga) + moat, four cardinal gates, palace/temple at Brahmasthana, raja-marga, occupational zoning, NE water body, SE/SW heavy zones, S cremation grounds outside walls.
  4. Examples — Madurai (Padmaka), Jaipur (9-pada mandala, 1727), Pataliputra, Harappa.
End with: these principles delivered hygiene, drainage, defence, and aesthetic harmony — concepts modern urban planners now rediscover under "biophilic" and "human-scale" design.
Q3. What are the eight types of town plans?
Dandaka, Sarvatobhadra, Nandyavarta, Padmaka, Swastika, Prastara, Karmuka, Chaturmukha.
Q4. What is the Brahmasthana?
The central 9 squares of the Vastu Purusha Mandala — sacred space kept open as a courtyard / skylight. Symbolises the navel of the cosmic Vastu-Purusha.

03 Medicinal Traditions — Ayurveda Unit IV · 3 Hrs

Unit IV centres on Ayurveda: definition, origin, the Tridosha theory, and the comparison with Allopathy. May-2025 hit it twice — a 1.5-mark short, plus a 5+10 split inside Q3.

3.1 What is Ayurveda?

Definition Ayurveda = Ayuh (life) + Veda (knowledge / science) → "the science of life." It is the indigenous Indian system of medicine, considered an Upaveda of the Atharvaveda, and dates to ~1500 BCE in oral form.

Origin & relationship to the Vedas

  • Hymns on healing herbs occur in the Rigveda & especially the Atharvaveda.
  • Codified later in two foundational texts:
    • Charaka Samhita — Charaka, ~1st c. CE — internal medicine (Kayachikitsa).
    • Sushruta Samhita — Sushruta, ~6th c. BCE — surgery (Shalya-tantra); calls Sushruta the "father of surgery."
    • Ashtanga Hridaya & Ashtanga Sangraha — Vagbhata, ~7th c. CE — concise compendia.
  • Together these are called the Brihat Trayi (Great Triad).

3.2 The eight branches — Ashtanga Ayurveda

3.3 Sutrasthana — the foundation

Definition Each Samhita is divided into 8 sthanas; the Sutrasthana ("section of aphorisms") is the first & most important — laying out fundamental theory: definition of life (ayu), philosophy, doshas, dhatus, dietetics, daily routine (dinacharya), seasonal routine (ritucharya), and ethics of the physician.
Famous Sutrasthana excerpts "Hitahitam sukham duhkham ayustasya hitahitam, manam cha tachcha yatroktam ayurvedah sa uchyate."
Translation: "That which deals with the beneficial & harmful, the happy & sorrowful aspects of life and the measure of life — is called Ayurveda." (Charaka Samhita 1.1.41)

3.4 Tridosha — three biological humours

Definition Health = balance of three doshas (humours). Imbalance (vikriti) causes disease. Each dosha is composed of two of the five mahabhutas.

3.7 Distinct features of Ayurveda

  1. Prakriti analysis — every individual is born with a unique dosha balance; treatment is personalised.
  2. Holism — body + mind + atma + indriya treated together.
  3. Dinacharya & Ritucharya — daily & seasonal routines codified.
  4. Panchakarma — five purification therapies: Vamana, Virechana, Basti, Nasya, Raktamokshana.
  5. Ahara as medicine — diet itself is treatment ("when diet is right, medicine is of no use").
  6. Rasayana & Vajikarana — entire branches dedicated to longevity & reproductive vitality.

Quick Quiz — Unit IV

Q1. What is Ayurveda and how is it related to the Vedas? PYQ 2025 · 1.5
Ayurveda = "science of life" (ayuh + veda) — the holistic Indian medical system. It is an Upaveda of the Atharvaveda; healing hymns appear in the Rig & Atharvavedas, and were codified into the Charaka, Sushruta, and Ashtanga Hridaya samhitas (the Brihat Trayi).
Q2. Compare Ayurveda with Allopathy in terms of diagnosis, treatment and principles. PYQ · 10 marks
Use a 4-column comparison + 1 paragraph synthesis:
  1. Principle: Ayurveda — holistic balance of tridosha; Allopathy — reductionist organ/cell focus.
  2. Diagnosis: Ayurveda — Trividha (Darshan/Sparshan/Prashna) + Ashtavidha (nadi, mutra, jihva ...); Allopathy — labs & imaging.
  3. Treatment: Ayurveda — herbs, Panchakarma, diet, lifestyle, prakriti-personalised; Allopathy — synthetic drugs, surgery, standardised protocols.
  4. Strengths/weaknesses: Ayurveda excels in chronic + preventive care; Allopathy excels in acute + emergency care.
Conclude: integrative medicine uses both — chronic disease management with Ayurveda, surgical/infectious care with Allopathy.

Q4. Name the three doshas and one disease imbalance for each.
Vata (air + ether) — anxiety, joint pain. Pitta (fire + water) — ulcers, inflammation. Kapha (earth + water) — obesity, congestion.

04 Indian Mathematics & Astronomy Unit III · 8 Hrs

The single largest unit (8 Hrs). May-2025 squeezed at least 3 PYQ short answers + a 5-mark Vedic computation question from here. The big themes: zero & place value, Vedic Sutras, Sulbasutras geometry, and the Aryabhata–Bhaskara astronomy lineage.

4.1 Why Indian mathematics matters

Definition Indian mathematics gave the world the decimal place-value system with a symbol for zero (shunya), the foundations of algebra (bijaganita) & trigonometry (jyotpatti), and the infinite series for π & sine centuries before Newton/Leibniz.

4.2 Arithmetic — decimal & zero

  • Place value — first stated explicitly by Aryabhata: "sthana sthana dasha-gunam syat" (each place is ten times the previous).
  • Shunya (zero) — first treated as a number with operational rules by Brahmagupta:
    • a + 0 = a, a × 0 = 0, a − 0 = a.
    • (He erred only on a ÷ 0 — left it undefined; the modern result.)
  • Negative numbers — Brahmagupta called positive "dhana" (fortune) and negative "rina" (debt) — gave full sign rules.
  • Brahmi numerals evolved → Indo-Arabic → today's 0–9.

4.3 Vedic mathematics — 16 sutras (PYQ — 5 marks)

Definition "Vedic Mathematics" was reconstructed by Swami Bharati Krishna Tirtha (1884–1960) into 16 sutras + 13 sub-sutras. Each sutra is a one-line aphorism that turns a complex calculation into a near-mental shortcut.

4.4 Geometry — Sulbasutras & the Baudhayana theorem

Definition The Sulbasutras (~800 BCE) are appendices to the Kalpa-Vedanga that tell priests how to construct vedi (fire altars) of precise area. They contain the earliest statements of the Pythagoras theorem & geometric area-preservation.
Baudhayana Sulbasutra 1.12 (~800 BCE) "Dirghachatursrasyaaksnaya rajjuh parshvamani tiryagmani cha yat prithagbhute kurutastadubhayam karoti."
"The diagonal-rope of an oblong produces both — the area produced separately by its breadth-rope and length-rope." → a² + b² = c², well before Pythagoras.

Other Sulbasutra contributions:

  • Construction of squares, circles, trapezia of given area.
  • Approximation: √2 ≈ 1 + 1/3 + 1/(3·4) − 1/(3·4·34) ≈ 1.41421568 (correct to 5 decimals).
  • Squaring the circle (approximate).

4.5 Trigonometry & the sine function

  • Aryabhata named the half-chord Ardha-jya → jya → jiba (Arab transliteration) → sinus (Latin) → sine. Word "sine" is etymologically Sanskrit.
  • Aryabhata's jya table (sine table) for every 3.75° from 0° to 90°.
  • Bhaskara II — sine of sums, half-angle formulas.
  • Madhava (Kerala school, ~1400 CE) — infinite series:
    Madhava — Infinite Seriesπ/4 = 1 − 1/3 + 1/5 − 1/7 + 1/9 − ... (Madhava–Leibniz, 250 yrs before Leibniz) sin(x) = x − x³/3! + x⁵/5! − ... (Madhava, 300 yrs before Newton)

4.6 Spherical geometry & calculus seeds

  • Spherical trigonometry — Aryabhata & later Bhaskara II — needed for astronomy on the celestial sphere.
  • Calculus seeds — Bhaskara II's Siddhanta Shiromani shows the concept of instantaneous velocity (tatkalika gati) — a derivative in spirit, ~500 yrs before Newton.
  • Madhava + Nilakantha (Kerala school) — infinite series & convergence, the conceptual bridge to calculus.

Contributions: (i) Pancha-Siddhantika — digest of 5 astronomical schools (Surya, Romaka, Paulisa, Vasishtha, Paitamaha); (ii) Brihat Samhita — encyclopedic work covering astronomy, astrology, vastu, gemology, agriculture; (iii) Brihat Jataka — foundational horoscopy text. Q4. Highlight the contribution of Patanjali, Agasthya and Kanad. PYQ 2025 · 1.5

Agastya — Vedic seer credited with bringing learning to South India, foundational Tamil Sangam grammarian, contributions to medicine (Agastya Samhita) & siddha tradition. Kanada (Maharishi Kanad) — founder of the Vaisheshika school of philosophy; postulated atomic theory (paramanu) ~600 BCE — matter composed of indivisible atoms — 2400 yrs before Dalton.
Q5. What is the kuttaka method?
Aryabhata's algorithm for solving linear indeterminate equations ax + by = c in integers (Diophantine equation). It anticipates the modern Extended Euclidean Algorithm.
Q6. Differentiate Pre & Post Siddhantic astronomy.
Pre-Siddhantic (Vedanga Jyotisha, ~1400–500 BCE): calendric, ritual-driven, lunar months & nakshatras, no planetary models. Post-Siddhantic / Siddhantic (~400 CE +): mathematical, planetary orbits, sine tables, eclipse prediction, spherical geometry — works like Surya Siddhanta, Aryabhatiya, Brahmasphutasiddhanta, Siddhanta Shiromani.

  • Sama — conciliation, persuasion, sweet words.
  • Dana — gift, concession, bribery.

  • Danda — punishment / use of force (last resort).

  • Themes: virtues, duty, restraint, wise counsel, friendship & enemies.
  • Complements Arthashastra: Artha = "what to do"; Niti = "how to behave".

Q7 (8M) · Vedic Mathematics — 16 Sutras & the Indian System of Computation

Definition: "Vedic Mathematics" is the system of high-speed mental arithmetic compiled by Sri Bharati Krishna Tirtha (Shankaracharya of Puri) in the early 20th century from the parishishta of the Atharvaveda, published posthumously in 1965. It comprises 16 sutras + 13 sub-sutras covering all four arithmetic operations, algebra & basic geometry.

Some flagship sutras

Sutra (Sanskrit)TranslationApplication
Ekadhikena Purvena"By one more than the previous one"Squares of numbers ending in 5; recurring decimals
Nikhilam Navataścaramaṁ Daśataḥ"All from 9 and the last from 10"Multiplication near a base (e.g., 97×96)
Urdhva-Tiryagbhyam"Vertically and crosswise"General single-line multiplication of any two numbers
Paravartya Yojayet"Transpose and apply"Division, simple equations
Sankalana-Vyavakalanabhyam"By addition and subtraction"Solving simultaneous linear equations
Yavadunam Tavadunikrtya Varga Yojayet"Whatever the deficiency, lessen further by it & set up the square"Squares of numbers near a base

Worked example — Ekadhikena for 75²

75² using Ekadhikena Purvena Step 1 : the previous digit of 5 is 7 Step 2 : "one more than 7" = 8 Step 3 : multiply 7 × 8 = 56 Step 4 : append 25 (always for ...5² ) 75² = 5625 Same trick: 35² = 1225 ; 95² = 9025 — instant!

Why it matters

  • Speed: mental arithmetic 5–10× faster than long-form methods.
  • Pattern thinking: trains lateral problem-solving; valuable for CAT/GRE/UPSC.
  • Cross-validation: multiple sutras give multiple checks for the same answer.
  • Recognition: NCERT & CBSE pilot programmes; UK & Singapore schools have adopted modules.

Sanskrit excerpt: "Sthanat sthanam dasha-gunam syat" — "From place to place, the value is ten-fold" — the place-value principle that underlies the whole Vedic system.

Modern relevance: Vedic-Math curricula are now run by the Vedic Maths Forum India; researchers use it in algorithm design (e.g., faster multipliers in VLSI by Urdhva-Tiryagbhyam) — an active area in computer-arithmetic literature.

Conclusion: The 16 sutras crystallise an elegant Indian computational tradition that is being rediscovered for both education and high-speed digital arithmetic.

Q1. Name Kautilya's seven limbs (Saptanga) of the State.
Swami, Amatya, Janapada, Durga, Kosha, Danda, Mitra. Mnemonic: "Sw-Am-Ja-Du-Ko-Da-Mi".
Q2. Which mathematician first treated zero as a number with arithmetic rules?
Brahmagupta, in Brahmasphutasiddhanta (628 CE).
Q3. State the three doshas and their elements.
Vata (Air + Ether) · Pitta (Fire + Water) · Kapha (Earth + Water).
Q4. Which three temple styles characterise Indian architecture, and give one example each?
Nagara (Khajuraho), Dravida (Brihadeshwara, Tanjavur), Vesara (Hoysaleshwara, Halebidu).
Q5. Name the four Upayas of statecraft.
Sama (conciliation), Dana (gift), Bheda (division), Danda (force).
Q6. State the Vedic-Math sutra used to find 75².
"Ekadhikena Purvena" — "By one more than the previous one." 7×8 = 56, append 25 → 5625.
Q7. Who authored the Pancha-Siddhantika and what does it compile?
Varahamihira (~550 CE). It compiles the five prevailing astronomical schools: Surya, Romaka, Vasishtha, Paulisha, Paitamaha Siddhantas.
Q8. What does the centre (Brahma-sthana) of the Vastu mandala represent?
The seat of Brahma / source of prana; kept open to admit light & air — typically a courtyard.
